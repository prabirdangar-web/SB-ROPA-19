2. ROPA-1998 Pre-2006 Fixation (Always Frozen on 31-03-2009)

10/18/20-Year CAS Rule (ROPA-1998 phase): One additional increment is granted on completion of 10 and 20 years of service (in the same old scale). On completion of 18 years, staff drawing GP 1700 / 2600 / 3900 / 4100 (i.e. eligible for the 18-year scale change 1700→1800, 2600→2900, 3900→4100, 4100→4400) get one additional increment in the old scale first, and are then placed at the equal/next-higher stage of the new scale, subject to the ROPA-1998 minimum entry pay of the new scale. GP 4700/4800/5400 are not eligible for the 18-year scale change (no higher old-scale mapping exists). If LWP days are entered, every increment/CAS date on or after the "LWP Taken From Date" is deferred by that many days, since dies-non/LWP period does not count towards qualifying service for increment.

Completed-year (termination) date rule: if the date of joining is the 1st of a month, a completed year of service falls on that same date N years later. If the date of joining falls anywhere from the 2nd to the last day of a month, that part-month is rounded up as a full qualifying month, so the completed year falls on the last day of the month that is N years after the joining month — not the exact day-anniversary. From the following year onward this gives a clean 12-month span (1st of that month to the end of the 12th month).

3. ROPA-2009 Notional Fixation (Always Frozen on 31-12-2019)

LWP / Notional Increment Rule (G.O. No. 181-SE(B) Dt. 8.10.2009, ROPA-2009 & ROPA-2019 phases): Extraordinary Leave Without Pay (E.O.L.) on medical grounds does not defer the increment at all. For LWP on any other ground: leave of less than 1 month has no effect. Leave of 1 month or more defers the financial benefit of the increment (the notional 1-July date itself stays fixed and future increment dates continue on the normal cycle): if the LWP period ends before the 1-July date, the financial benefit starts from 1-July plus the number of complete LWP months; if the LWP period is still running on (i.e. spans across) the 1-July date, the financial benefit instead starts from the 1st of the month following the month of rejoining duty, plus the number of complete LWP months. If the LWP runs 12 months or more and swallows the 1-July date entirely, that year's increment is not granted at all and the employee simply resumes on the following year's 1-July cycle.
